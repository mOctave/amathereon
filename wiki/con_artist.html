Con Artist

    Class Data
    Con Artist
    Wide Class Rogue
    Skill Points 4
    Specials Shadowy Connections, Interpersonal Skills
    Languages Thieves' Cant
    Ability Score Bonuses +1 DEX, +2 CHA

    Con artists walk the streets of just about every city on Amathereon, trying to trick unwary tourists into losing their hard-earned, easily spent gold. These crafty criminals almost never get caught: their plans are well-laid and no one realizes that something has gone wrong at first. In many ways, they are like merchants: they can convince someone to buy a glass "diamond," and, when the disgruntled customer comes back demanding a refund, they'll find a way to avoid giving it to them, likely by being in the next town already.

    Shadowy Connections

    Con artists, like all Rogues, always have connections with the criminal underworld. As such, other people of low repute tend to trust them, and will give them information or even aid in exchange for enough gold.

    Interpersonal Skills

    Con artists receive +20% effect to uses of Mental: Deception, Mental: Intimidation, Mental: Performance, and Mental: Persuasion.

    Thieves' Cant

    Con artists learn how to speak Thieves' Cant, and can usually write it phonetically using the Common character set.

    Ability Score Bonuses

    Rogues gain a +1 bonus to dexterity and charisma.

    Con artists gain an additional +1 bonus to charisma, for a total of +2.

    Skills

    Rogues gain a level in each of the following skills: Knowledge, Mental, Mental: Deception, Mental: Performance, Physical: Acrobatics, Weapons, and Weapons: Piercing.

    Con artists gain an additional level in Knowledge: Culture, Mental: Deception, Mental: Intimidation, Mental: Performance, and Mental: Persuasion.

    Overall, con artists have 2 levels in knowledge skills, 7 levels in mental skills, 1 level in physical skills, and 2 levels in weapon skills.

    Con artists also receive an additional 4 skill points to spend.
