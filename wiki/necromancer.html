    Necromancers often have a bad reputation among others, and not just magicians. Despite this, there is very little truth to the tales people tell of their "evil rites." Necromancers do indeed commune with the souls of the dead, but there is little mysticism to it, just a brief superimposing of a specific subset of Entropy onto Reality. It is also untrue that the souls can escape into Reality: they are bound to Entropy and will forceably be returned within a few hours from the perspective of someone experiencing Perception.

    Nonetheless, there certainly are some very chaotic necromancers, who embrace the stereotypes of their kind and try their best to set the souls of particular people at free in Reality once more â€” under their control, of course. Thankfully, these dark necromancers have not succeeded in accomplishing these goals, nor will they ever.

    Necromancy

    Necromancers can superimpose part of Entropy onto Reality, and can communicate with the souls in that section. They can also draw power from that portion of Entropy for their own magic.

    Select a Languages

    Sorcerers can choose one additional language in which they can both speak, read, and write. This language cannot be Thieves' Cant or Druidic.

    Ability Score Bonuses

    Mages gain a +1 bonus to intelligence and wisdom.

    Sorcerers gain an additional +1 bonus to constitution.

    Skills

    Mages gain a level in each of the following skills: Knowledge: Arcana, Knowledge: Linguistics, Knowledge: Medicine, Mental, Mental: Perception, Mental: Performance, and Physical: Sleight of Hand.

    Necromancers gain an additional level in Knowledge, Knowledge: History, Mental: Performance, Physical: Sleight of Hand, and Weapons: Improvised.

    Overall, necromancers have 5 levels in knowledge skills, 4 levels in mental skills, 2 levels in physical skills, and 1 level in weapon skills.

    Necromancers also receive an additional 5 skill points to spend.
