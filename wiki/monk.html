Monk

    Class Data
    Monk
    Wide Class Specialist
    Skill Points 6
    Specials Unarmed Combat
    Languages None
    Ability Score Bonuses +1 DEX, +1 AGI, +1 RES

    There are many kinds of monk, from many kinds of religion, but almost all have some basic knowledge of how to protect themselves. In many religions, the monks guard the clerics and acolytes, leaving them free to pursue their work wherever it takes them. Many find it strange that such a large group under religious vows are still such powerful combatants, but it is perhaps not extraordinary considering that many of them were soldiers, criminals, or simple farmers before taking the vows that made them monks.

    Many monks develop and teach martial arts involving nothing more than their bare hands. While these arts are primarily intended as a way to improve control over one's body, they are still useful in combat situations, and have led to monks' reputations as people not to be messed with.

    Unarmed Combat

    Monks receive a +3 hit bonus, a +3 parry bonus, and a +1 damage bonus in unarmed combat or while using improvised weapons.

    Ability Score Bonuses

    Monks gain a +1 bonus to dexterity, agility, and resilience.

    Skills

    Specialists gain a level in each of the following skills: Knowledge, Mental, Physical, and Weapons.

    Monks gain an additional level in Knowledge: Culture, Knowledge: History, Knowledge: Linguistics, Mental: Perception, Physical: Acrobatics, Weapons: Improvised, Weapons: Melee and Weapons: Trauma.

    Overall, monks have 4 levels in knowledge skills, 2 levels in mental skills, 2 levels in physical skills, and 4 levels in weapon skills.

    Monks also receive an additional 6 skill points to spend.
